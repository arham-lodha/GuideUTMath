  first-relationship: |
    How did your first real relationship with a UT professor start? (~200 words)

    My first relationship with a professor started after I cold-emailed them and started to do research with them properly. I basically sent a short email saying I was interested in condensed matter and wanted to learn more, and that I was willing to start small. After that, I started sitting in on group meetings and watching student lectures and eventually did experiments and led some of the group meetings, before switching groups and having to do all of this over and over again.

  reading-course-format: |
    Reading courses: how did yours work? (~250 words)

    I did a reading course in the physics department with a PI/mentor. I read papers related to his research, namely topological band theory. It was pretty informal, mostly reading papers, meeting to talk through them, asking questions when I got stuck, and using that to build enough background to understand the research direction better. I assume math is isomorphic. Tho they def do not need to be your PI.

  letter-writers: |
    Who wrote your grad school letters, and how did you pick them? (~200 words)

    Junyeong Ahn: I wrote a paper with him, my senior thesis with him, I've been with his group for the better part of 2 years. I enjoyed working with him, and I think he thinks I am somewhat intelligent and capable.

    Scott Aaronson: I took two classes with him, got As in both, graded for his class, worked on a project in one of his classes that got featured in his blog. He also offered to write it.

    Luqiao Liu: My summer PI at MIT. He offered to write one, and I published a paper with him. I did a lot of work in modeling his experiments accurately and making new predictions for future research.

  ask-timing: |
    How and when did you ask? (~150 words)

    I asked spring junior year if they would be willing, and I reconfirmed early fall, and sent the letters late fall. I went in person when I could, otherwise emailed if they're halfway across the country.

  relationship-advice: |
    What do you wish you'd known earlier about building these relationships? (~200 words)

    Start early, go the extra mile, be a bit impulsive especially if they aren't super old. Make the first move. They're human and often times only slightly less socially awkward than you in the best case. So just try and put effort.

  ut-specific-note: |
    UT-specific note: which faculty or groups would you point a current undergrad toward, and for what? (~200 words)

    I would recommend theoretical physics groups. I guess some CS groups are also math-heavy, and there's maybe a proper math group that accepts undergrads. But the math in theoretical physics fulfills me enough. It does require a decent amount of physics knowledge, but physics is a fun major.
