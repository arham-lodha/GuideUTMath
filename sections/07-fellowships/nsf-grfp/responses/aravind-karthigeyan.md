# Aravind Karthigeyan on the NSF GRFP

*Written April 2026, graduated Fall 2025, now at UIUC Theoretical Physics PhD.*  
*Applied 2026, outcome awarded.*

## What was your research proposal about, in plain language? (~200 words)

My research was about topological qubits. Using some recent experimental advancements in physics, a new type of topological qubit could technically exist. I would use some math, especially K-Theory, to actually model how the physical structure in the first place would form the qubits in the first place. Then I would use some knot theory, specifically homotopy of braid groups, to analyze the resulting anyons and how to properly measure, apply gates, and control them for quantum computations. My final step was talking to experimentalists and figuring out the major ways to design it, possible hurdles, and how to do it efficiently.

## How did you land on that topic, given when you applied? (~200 words)

I landed on the topic based on my interests and doing a lot of research. My main interests and history of research were in topological condensed matter theory and quantum computing. I also read a lot of papers and just thought quite a bit. I realized a certain experimental result would allow the perfect combination of physically realizable systems to have a mathematically interesting quantum state that can actually be used for something quite important to the government.

## What was the argument of your personal statement? (~250 words)

It was very chronological for the first half: how I got interested in my research and my main research experiences. I tried to make it almost like a story to entice them. My first paragraph was why I was interested in the field and what I find beautiful. The first half was a chronological research story thingy-majig. I also talked about what I would like to do in the future to help out my graduate community and school itself.

## What did you do about broader impacts? (~200 words)

For my research proposal, my broader impacts were talking about how I would make a new type of topological qubit. Especially since a lot of current topological qubits aren't very well realized or are hard to verify or hard to make for a lot of groups. I made one that would ideally be relatively easy to make, and it's just another device design, which is good. Topological quantum is also another way of quantum computing that is of interest due to error correction.

For my personal statement, I talked about what I would do in grad school, especially because of how active I was in undergrad. I would continue to mentor, help out in grad orgs, help teach, start new programs related to my research, do outreach, etc.

## Who read your drafts, and how many drafts did you go through? (~150 words)

Just my PI. I made 5 drafts where I emphasized different styles by looking at successful GRFPs that were online. I saw which ones go best with each other and slowly merged my best together, removing parts I didn't like, adding ones I did, redoing entire parts, and eventually landing on one I liked and showed my PI.
