# Fellowships

**Format:** structured responses per fellowship.

## What goes here

- NSF GRFP
- Churchill Scholarship
- Other fellowships people applied for — successful or not. Unsuccessful applications are often more instructive than successful ones; please write them up if you applied.

## Format

Each fellowship has its own subsection and its own set of questions. Contributors answer in their own voice. Responses sit side-by-side.


## NSF GRFP contributor questions

Copy this into `nsf-grfp/responses/yourname.md`.

```
# [Your name] on the NSF GRFP

*Written [month year], graduated [year], now at [where].*  
*Applied [year], outcome [awarded / honorable mention / declined].*

## What was your research proposal about, in plain language? (~200 words)

[Enough that a junior can understand what you proposed, without requiring the technical background.]

## How did you land on that topic, given when you applied? (~200 words)

[This is the question most applicants struggle with. Were you already working on it? Did you pick something adjacent to your REU? Did you write about something you hadn't actually started?]

## What was the argument of your personal statement? (~250 words)

[What story were you telling about yourself, and how did you tie it to broader impacts?]

## What did you do about broader impacts? (~200 words)

[The part everyone complains about. How did you handle it?]

## Who read your drafts, and how many drafts did you go through? (~150 words)

## What would you do differently? (~200 words)

## Advice to a junior thinking about applying in the fall. (~200 words)
```

## Churchill Scholarship contributor questions

Copy this into `churchill/responses/yourname.md`.

```
# [Your name] on the Churchill Scholarship

*Written [month year], graduated [year], now at [where].*  
*Applied [year], outcome [scholar / finalist / not advanced].*

## What is the Churchill actually, and why did you apply? (~200 words)

[Many UT math majors have never heard of it. Start at the beginning.]

## What was the application like, timeline included? (~300 words)

[Campus endorsement process, national application, essays, interviews. What surprised you?]

## What did your research project proposal look like? (~250 words)

## What did the interview process involve? (~200 words)

## Who is the Churchill a good fit for, and who should skip it? (~200 words)

## What would you do differently? (~150 words)
```

## Other fellowships

If you applied for any of the following — Goldwater, Rhodes, Marshall, Gates Cambridge, Knight-Hennessy, Hertz, DOE CSGF, NDSEG, etc. — please write it up even briefly. Use the NSF GRFP question template as a starting point and adapt.
