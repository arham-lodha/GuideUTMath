# Paths through UT math

*Draft, to be updated as contributors submit bios. Maintained by the steward.*

This is a quick orientation to the different shapes an undergrad math career at UT can take, with pointers to contributors who walked each one. If you see a path that resembles what you're imagining for yourself, jump to that contributor's bio and structured responses first.

## Came in knowing the math they loved

Some people walked into UT already knowing they were into topology, or algebraic geometry, or number theory, and built their undergrad around it. This is less common than it looks from the outside and doesn't correlate as strongly with eventual success as you might think — but if this is you, you exist.


## Figured it out late

Much more common. You spent three years getting stronger at math without being sure what you liked about it, and the answer came together sometime around the start of senior year — or it still hasn't fully, and you applied anyway.

Examples: Arham (number theory / representation theory, landed late, still open to analytic directions; UCLA). If this is you: the structured responses in [`sections/06-grad-school/`](../06-grad-school/) are the ones to read first.

## Adjacent-field PhDs

You did math, but your center of gravity drifted toward physics, CS, neuroscience, or something else, and you're applying to PhDs in that adjacent field rather than pure math.

## Fell out of love with math

This happens, it's not a failure, and it's one of the most underwritten-about paths. 

## Paths not represented in this cohort

We don't currently have contributors who went directly into industry software, quantitative finance, actuarial work, secondary-school teaching, or data science out of undergrad. These are all real and reasonable paths for a UT math graduate. If you took one, please write.
