# Other paths

**Format:** individual essays.

Not everyone who majors in math at UT goes into a math PhD, and this guide would be dishonest if it implied otherwise. This section is for the paths that run adjacent to, alongside, or away from pure math.

## What goes here


## Format

Individual essays, 800–1500 words, in your own voice. Suggested structure below — use it or don't.

## Suggested structure

- The path in one sentence.
- When you first suspected this was where you were headed, and when you knew.
- What you did as an undergrad that mattered for this path (and what you did that turned out not to matter).
- What the pivot (if any) looked like, including how you handled telling people — advisors, family, yourself.
- What you wish you'd known about this path as a freshman or sophomore.
- Who this path is a good fit for. Who it isn't.
- What a pure-math-oriented math major should know about the overlap between their path and yours — what skills transfer, what doesn't, what would have helped you.

## A note on framing

These aren't consolation-prize paths. They're paths. Please write them that way. A masters or a pivot to a different field isn't a bad thing, it's a considered choice that a real person made, and a future reader trying to decide what to do with their own life deserves to hear it told straight.
