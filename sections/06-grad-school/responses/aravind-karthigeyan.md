  list-building: |
    Where did you apply, and roughly how did you build the list? (~250 words)

    I applied for 18 schools, with roughly the best faculty in topological condensed matter theory. I asked my PI if my list was fine, and he said that he wouldn't make any changes.

  letters: |
    Who wrote your letters, how did you pick them, and when did you ask? (~250 words)

    My main PI at UT Austin, my summer REU PI, and a professor I took a couple classes with, graded for, and did a research project under. They were the current professors I was closest to relationship-wise. I asked at the end of spring for my non-REU profs, and the end of summer for my REU one. More like they offered me, but I properly formally asked them in person in fall and emailed the MIT one. I sent the emails late fall.

  sop: |
    What was the core argument of your statement of purpose? (~300 words)

    It was pretty chronological and story-like. Why my interests eventually became condensed matter theory and quantum information. Then some stuff about why I would be a good fit and why I like these certain professors and why I like to teach, help the community, etc. whatnot.

  gre: |
    GRE and Math GRE: what did you do, and would you do it again? (~150 words)

    I took the Physics GRE. n = 1, but I feel like it was decently important, in the sense that it might've pushed me over the edge for some programs when they were basically deciding which condensed matter theorists to recommend for admission.

  interviews: |
    Interviews and visits: what were they actually like? (~200 words)

    I had 3 interviews. One went super duper well. We just vibed, she got excited. I got put on a shortlist immediately. Others were fine, but I didn't get in for those. For the colleges I visited, UIUC had the best grad student culture and the best overall research. Other places usually had one worse than the other. Lots of bar nights too!!! Got into 6 places, so yeah. Very intense month.

  decision: |
    How did you make your final decision? (~250 words)

    The place with the best research also had the best grad student culture. Location isn't super duper great, but worth it.

  mistakes: |
    What did you get wrong or waste time on? (~200 words)

    Maybe reach out to profs? Doesn't hurt? I didn't do it and was mostly fine, but I've heard it works.

  advice-to-self: |
    One thing you'd tell yourself in September of senior year. (~100 words)

    To just push through a bit more. End is in sight, just a few more months.
