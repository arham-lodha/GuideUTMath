# Xinbo Li on grad school applications

*Written April 2026, graduated 2026, now at Duke University.*

## Where did you apply, and roughly how did you build the list? (~250 words)

I applied to 25 schools, which is a lot! I used the US News ranking in math to list the top 50 schools in math. Then for each of them I look for their faculties to see if they have enough geometry people for my interest. "Enough" means at least two for each of my interests. I also asked my recommenders for some schools.

## Who wrote your letters, how did you pick them, and when did you ask? (~250 words)

A professor who knows me *really* well and taught five courses I took (including a reading course), a professor who is well-known in his field and taught two courses I took, a professor who taught me two courses and I went to all his office hours (so he knows me well too), and one of my mentors in a research project. I asked three of them at the beginning of the Fall semester of my senior year. The other professor I was too shy to ask so I procrasinated this task around a week before my earliest applications are due.

## What was the core argument of your statement of purpose? (~300 words)

I want to find a job in academia so a Ph.D. is a good move. I have done serious coursework and some research in math and I "know what I am talking about".

## GRE and Math GRE: what did you do, and would you do it again? (~150 words)

I did both. I only studied for the Math GRE because many department either "strongly recommend" or require it, while most departments do not need the standard GRE. I would not waste my money on standard GRE again.

## Interviews and visits: what were they actually like? (~200 words)

I only got interviewed by Duke. They asked questions about my research and my interests in math (because I listed a lot of interests in my SOP). I only visited to Duke and basically it's just a day's tour on campus and meet the faculties (especially people who you want to work with) and the graduate students.

## How did you make your final decision? (~250 words)

I only got accepted to one so it's easy.

## What did you get wrong or waste time on? (~200 words)

Fixing the pronouns of my recommenders, and checking my application portal again and again even when they didn't send out anything yet. Also doom scrolling GradCafe posts that destroys my mental.

## One thing you'd tell yourself in September of senior year. (~100 words)

Don't think of yourself too low.
