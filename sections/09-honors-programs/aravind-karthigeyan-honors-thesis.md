# Aravind on an honors thesis

## Mechanics

My research PI, Junyeong Ahn, supervised me. I was doing research with him for over a semester beforehand, and we were writing a paper, so it was a no-brainer. It was my senior fall. 3 hours. No defense. I did a physics thesis, so I needed to email the top physics advisor and that was basically it.

## Choosing an advisor and topic

My advisor picked the topic I was working on a while before. I think it was an interesting problem that stumped him and a few other people when he was a grad student. It came directly from my earlier research. I wish I had read some examples beforehand. There weren't a lot available on ScholarWorks, the repository for such theses.

## What the thesis actually was

My thesis was about probing the second Stiefel-Whitney class and being able to detect this topological invariant due to oscillations in the resistivity of a semimetal under a strong magnetic field. It was original research and I later made it into a much higher quality paper. I used some homotopy theory and real Grassmannians to properly define the second Stiefel-Whitney class in terms of real vector bundles and bands for my material. I made a model toy Hamiltonian. I did some many-body physics, basically accounting for certain types of Feynman diagrams, and linear response theory to find the said leading-order properties of the system, namely the conductivity when it was under a magnetic field. I had to use a lot of annoying but satisfying magnetic translation algebra. I ended up showing we can indeed detect the second Stiefel-Whitney class with a strong magnetic field by showing oscillations in resistivity. So this compound could be topologically trivial according to Chern-Simons theory but still have a topological invariant due to symmetry.

## Was it worth the time?

Yes. It helped me develop ideas much quicker since there was a proper time crunch. It helped me write down and organize a lot of stuff, which was useful for paper writing. It helped me express my ideas and verify a lot of information, though it was stressful toward the end since I did delay writing it by quite a bit.
