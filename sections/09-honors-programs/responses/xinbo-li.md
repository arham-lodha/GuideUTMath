# Honors Tutorial Course (Honors Thesis)

## Registration and Choosing an Advisor

First, you go talk with a professor who you want to work with. Then if the professor agrees to do a reading course that leads to a thesis, you would email Dr. Theresa to register for the Honors tutorial course. It is a course with 3 credit hours and a writing flag. There is no defense. 

I didn't have a topic that I wanted to do before I went to Professor Perutz. He is very good at suggesting topics! I said I wanted to do something related to algebraic geometry. Although he is not an algebraic geometer, he suggested a topic that sits in between algebraic geometry and differential geometry. It turned out that I really liked the flavor of the subject!

My recommendation is to find a professor who knows *you* well enough, e.g. someone that you had at least took a course with and in which you were very active. They would be really good at suggesting topics that you may be interested in (and also they *know* about)!

## What My Thesis Is About

It is an expository paper on variation of complex structures. In the setting, one cares about how a family behaves instead of how a single object behaves --- sounds like moduli problems! It falls in the subject of deformation theory. 

Let me be a bit more specific. Say we have a family of complex manifolds with a chosen "base" complex manifold, i.e. a "reference manifold". For a family of complex manifolds there is always a good enough trivialization (maybe after restricting the family to only manifolds that are "close" to the base manifold) so that it induces diffeomorphisms (i.e. smooth equivalences) between these manifolds. However, these objects are complex manifolds, and diffeomorphisms don't preserve complex structures --- they only preserve smooth structures. But we can transport the complex structures from this family of complex manifolds to a family of complex structures on the base manifold using the diffeomorphisms. My thesis explores how we may describe the variation of this family of complex structures induces on the based manifold, and how different descriptions interact and coincide with each other.
