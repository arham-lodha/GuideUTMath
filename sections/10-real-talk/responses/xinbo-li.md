# On comparison

I want to say that there is no need to compare with other people in terms of the pace of taking courses, or in terms of publications. If you have friends that have taken a lot of advanced courses in their first couple of years and had published in some journals, good for them! But I came to realize as I become a senior that I am not quite competing with others in math, and let me clarify this. 

First I came to realize that everyone has different starting points, which directly affects how fast people are taking certain classes. I came in college with no prior exposure to linear algebra and didn't know how to do double/triple integrals while almost everyone else in my vector calculus knows how to do them! In my cohort there are people who had done some college level courses in algebra or analysis in high school, so it is not fair at all for me to compare to them in terms of mathematical maturity. 

Then I found out that people in my cohort had very different interests. Some of my friends are interested in analytic number theory, which I just simply cannot understand what's going on! Therefore it is also not fair to compare your mathematical intuition with your friends in other fields. But it is also bad to not talk to them at all about math.

# Mental Health in Graduate Application Season

After I submitted my applications, I started the stage of doom scrolling. I refresh the GradCafe pages everyday to see if anyone got back from the schools that I had applied to. It is very bad for your mental health and I advice strongly against doing so, but I also understand that it is impossible to not care, since getting a Ph.D. offer means a lot to your career as a mathematician. In my case, my family won't be able to sponsor any further studies financially, so I would really have to say goodbye to math if I fail this cycle. 

The best advice that I could give is to have some projects to work on to distract yourself from thinking about it. In my case, I forgot about applications best when I sit down and start working on math. 

# Pacing

In comparison with my peers, my pacing is slow. However I think this is beneficial. I strongly advice anyone to learn more advanced math only after they could work with what they learned so far. I am not saying that you should learn the subject perfectly --- as you learn more you will realize different perspectives of the subject which you are already familiar with. I am saying that people should at least listen to advices that other people say with respect to prerequisites. Some people burn out because they couldn't use their fundamentals good enough and this burden stacks over time. 

# One Sentence Summary

Math is not a competition --- take your time and I'm sure you will get wherever your interest takes you.
