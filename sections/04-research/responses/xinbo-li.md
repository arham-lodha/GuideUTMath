# Xinbo Li on research

*Written April 2026, graduated 2026, now at Duke University.*

## What research did you do as an undergrad, in what order? (~250 words)

**Texas Experimental Geometry Lab (TXGL)**: from Spring 2025 to Spring 2026. We focused on computing Cěch cohomology groups for tiling spaces, specifically square, chair, Penrose, and GLB tilings.

**Polymath Jr. REU**: Summer 2025. I studied Blaschke products and the geometry related to it. In particular, to each degree $n$ Blaschke product one can associate with it an *envelope*. I am interested in how the geometry of the envelope interacts with properties of Blaschke products.

## How did you get your first research experience? (~200 words)

I got an email about TXGL from Professor Danciger when I was taking my point-set topology class. I filled a questionaire and answered a (math) problem. Later I was luckily assigned for a project. 

## If you did an REU: how did you pick where to apply, and how did you decide between offers? (~250 words)

I applied to most REUs that take international students (most of the REU projects, due to funding, do not take international students). I believe there is a list of most REUs and I just clicked their official websites individually to see what's their requirement. I only got accepted to the Polymath Jr. REU, which seems to be welcoming to all undergraduates with a proof-writing experience. The downside though is that you are not paid and it is totally remote, due to its nature (mentors are from different institutions). 

## What did "doing research" actually look like day-to-day? (~200 words)

First I got through the foundational materials. After that it's basically try to crack a specific question by forming small questions that may or may not matter when you try to solve your chosen question! Sometimes to form intuition I need to compute examples, either by hand or by using Mathematica and Python. 

## What did you get out of your research experiences — honestly? (~200 words)

For practical purpose, I grinded hard enough to get a recommendation letter from one of my project mentors. I also got the chance to present, together with another project member, the group's work during the project at JMM 2026. I also got to know what real research is like -- most of the time you don't know the answer and you have to endure the process of not knowing stuff! 

## What did you do when you didn't like what you were doing? (~250 words)

I switch to new problems or directions if I find out I don't like what I am doing. I never force myself to fall in love with something I don't enjoy and I barely spend any time to these things. 

## One piece of tactical advice for someone trying to get into research. (~150 words)
